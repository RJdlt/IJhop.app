How I'd approach it

The simplest split: you stay on the things only you can do, and I take care of the practical online side. Once it runs, I show you how it works so you can manage the day to day yourself. I'm not looking to take anything over. I'd set it up properly, write down how it works, and hand it to you.

You

  • The products and how they're made
  • The look and the taste
  • What Pause stands for
  • Your customers

Me

  • The online shop and the setup behind it
  • The marketing and how people find you
  • Keeping it organised and measurable
  • Then showing you how to run it

What I'd set up

A few concrete things, none of them big or expensive to start with:

  • A proper online shop: clean, fast, and easy to buy from.
  • The brand basics, so everything looks consistent. Mostly that means good photos of the products, which matter more online than anything else.
  • The marketing setup: email to stay in touch with people who are interested, a simple presence on Pinterest and Instagram, and over time a few articles that help people find you on Google.
  • Simple tracking, so we can see what's actually working instead of guessing.

My honest take: it's better to start small and solid than big and half finished. We'd get the basics right first and add the rest as it makes sense.

If there's one thing worth doing first, it's good product photos. Online, that's what makes the biggest difference.

Roughly how it would go

No fixed timeline. We'd work at whatever pace suits you.

  • First, confirm the basics (products, prices, what you already have) and set up the shop.
  • Then, a quiet launch to people you know, fix whatever needs fixing, and a proper launch after that.
  • After that, keep the marketing ticking over and grow steadily, while I gradually hand things over to you.

If you want to take it further

To make it concrete I'd just need a sense of a few things: the products and prices, how they're made, where you'd like to sell, and what you already have (logo, photos, a current website).

Easiest first step is a short call. You tell me what you have in mind, I'll tell you honestly what I think is worth doing, and we go from there. That's it. Happy to help if it's useful.

Robert-Jan
