# Pause Interior — Online Marketing Playbook (First 90 Days)

> _Deliverable 04 · v1.0 · June 2026 · English-first_
> Built on [01-BUSINESS-CONCEPT.md](./01-BUSINESS-CONCEPT.md) §10. This is the
> concrete, do-this-now plan: the engine, the channels, the email flows (with
> copy), and a 90-day calendar — sized for a small, founder-led team.

---

## 1. The one strategic idea

> **Build owned assets that compound. Rent attention only to feed them.**

Most small brands pour money into ads and vanish the day they stop. Pause does
the opposite: every month we grow an **owned audience** (email list + content
library + community) that keeps selling for years. Paid is **fuel**, not the
engine.

```
PAID (fuel)  ──►  OWNED (engine)  ◄──  EARNED (word of mouth)
Pinterest         Website + Journal      Reviews, UGC, PR
Meta retarget     Email / SMS list       Referrals
Google Shopping   Community ("Circle")
```

**The 90-day north star:** _not_ vanity reach. Two numbers:
1. **Email list growth** (the compounding asset), and
2. **First-purchase conversion** (proof the funnel works).
Everything else is secondary for now.

---

## 2. The content engine — "one pause, many pieces"

The "magic" of consistent, beautiful marketing on a small team is a **system**,
not heroics. Each substantial idea is created once and atomised:

```
1 core idea / shoot ("Making a small bedroom feel calm")
   ├─ 1 Journal article            → SEO, long-life, owned
   ├─ 4–6 Instagram / Pinterest posts
   ├─ 1–2 short reels (slow, ambient)
   ├─ 1 email newsletter
   └─ 3–5 ad creatives (later)
```

> **Cadence that's sustainable:** 1 substantial "pillar" idea per **2 weeks** →
> feeds ~2 weeks of social + 1 newsletter + 1 article. Quality and consistency
> beat volume. Better to post 3×/week forever than 14×/week for a month.

## 3. Channel playbook

### 3.1 Email & SMS — _the money channel_ (priority #1)

Highest ROI in e-commerce, and **owned** — no algorithm in the way. Set up in
**Klaviyo**.

**Capture (turn traffic into a list):**
- Lead magnet: a genuinely useful, beautiful **"Calm Home" styling guide** (PDF)
  _or_ a gentle welcome offer. Trade it for an email.
- Newsletter sign-up in footer + delayed/exit pop-up (on-brand, §03-4.1).

**The 5 core automated flows (build before launch):**

1. **Welcome series (3 emails)** — the highest-revenue flow.
   - _Email 1 (immediate):_ deliver the guide + the founder's story (warmth + trust). One soft CTA.
   - _Email 2 (+2 days):_ the philosophy — _fewer, better things_; materials & sustainability.
   - _Email 3 (+4 days):_ the hero pieces / bestsellers + first gentle invitation to shop (+ welcome offer if used).
2. **Browse abandonment** — visited a product, didn't add. One reminder, story-led.
3. **Cart/checkout abandonment (2 emails)** — _+1h:_ gentle reminder + trust signals; _+24h:_ help/reassurance (delivery, returns, "questions? just reply").
4. **Post-purchase (2–3 emails)** — order confirmation (warm) → shipping → **after-care + review request + how to "pause" with your new piece**. This is where word-of-mouth is born.
5. **Win-back** — lapsed customers (~90 days): a quiet "we've been thinking of your home" + what's new.

**The newsletter ("Slow letters"):** every ~2 weeks. **Not** a sales blast — a
short, lovely note: a thought on slow living, one beautiful image, a styling
idea, maybe one product woven in. People should _want_ to open it. _(Voice: see
02-§3.2.)_

> ⚠️ **CONFIRM:** Klaviyo (or current ESP) access, and whether an email list
> already exists to import.

**Sample welcome Email 1 (copy, ready to adapt):**

> **Subject:** Welcome — take a pause
>
> Hej, and welcome.
>
> Pause began with a simple idea: that home should be the place your shoulders
> finally drop. Not a showroom. Not more stuff. Just fewer, better things — made
> from honest materials, designed to last, here in Sweden.
>
> Here's the Calm Home guide I made for you _[link]_. No rush. Read it with a
> coffee.
>
> Over the next few days I'll share a little of why we do this, and the pieces
> people love most. If anything ever catches your eye — or you just want to say
> hej — reply to this email. A real person (me) reads them.
>
> Take a pause,
> _[Founder name] · Pause Interior_

### 3.2 Pinterest — _the long-life discovery engine_ (priority #2)

Pinterest is a **visual search engine**, not social media: a pin drives traffic
for **months or years**, unlike a post that dies in a day. For interior buyers
in planning mode, it's the highest-leverage discovery channel.

- Set up a **business account**; verify the site; enable Rich Pins.
- Boards by theme: _Calm Bedrooms · Natural Materials · Scandinavian Living ·
  Small-Space Calm · The Pause Home._
- Pin every product (clean + lifestyle) and every Journal article, with
  keyword-rich, helpful descriptions.
- Aim for a steady pinning cadence (e.g. 5–10/week, mostly scheduled).

---

### 3.3 Instagram — _desire + brand world_ (priority #3)

Not "posting products" — **art-directing the feeling** (calm rooms, natural
light, tactile detail, the founder's world, slow moments).

- **Grid:** a tonal, cohesive mosaic — looks like one continuous mood (§02-6).
- **Reels:** slow, ambient, no frantic cuts (§02-5.3) — best organic reach.
- **Stories:** behind-the-scenes, materials, the human founder → builds intimacy.
- **Bio:** _Timeless Scandinavian interior, made to last. Designed in Sweden._ +
  link to site/lead magnet.
- Engagement > follower count. Reply to every comment/DM warmly.

---

### 3.4 Journal / SEO — _the compounding free-traffic asset_ (priority #4)

Articles that answer what the customer already googles, earning durable Google
traffic and positioning the founder as an authority. Each article links to
products and to the newsletter.

**Starter article list (buyer-intent + brand):**
1. _How to make a small bedroom feel calm_
2. _A guide to natural materials: oak, linen, wool, stone_
3. _Scandinavian styling: fewer, better things_
4. _How to create a "pause" corner at home_
5. _Caring for solid wood / linen / wool (so it lasts a lifetime)_
6. _What "designed in Sweden" really means_

---

### 3.5 Influencer & partnerships — _borrowed trust_ (Phase 2)

Seed product to a small number of **taste-aligned** micro-influencers / interior
accounts (calm, credible, on-brand). **Fit over follower count.** A gifted piece
in the right home is worth more than a paid post in the wrong one.

---

### 3.6 PR & earned media — _the founder story_ (Phase 2)

Pitch the **founder's story** and the "pause" philosophy to design and
slow-living press. A founder story is far more pitchable than a product. Build a
simple press kit (story, hi-res images, founder bio, key facts).

---

### 3.7 Paid acquisition — _controlled fuel_ (start small, Phase 2)

Only after the funnel converts organically. Order of spend (warmest/cheapest
first):
1. **Retargeting** (Meta + Pinterest) — people who already visited. Cheapest wins.
2. **Pinterest ads** — interior intent.
3. **Google Shopping / Search** — high-intent buyers searching the category.
4. **Meta prospecting** — only once creative + funnel are proven.

> **Rule:** scale only what shows positive **contribution margin after CAC**
> (see 05). Never scale a leaky funnel — fix conversion first.

## 4. The 90-day calendar

> Roles assumed: a founder (+ a designer/photographer and a part-time marketer or
> agency). Adjust to real capacity. ⚠️ **CONFIRM team & budget.**

### Month 1 — Foundation & soft launch
**Goal: assets + audience capture live.**

- **Week 1:** Core-collection **photo + video shoot** (the #1 asset, §02-5).
  Confirm brand assets. Set up Klaviyo, GA4, Pinterest, Search Console.
- **Week 2:** Build/upgrade the store (per 03). Write Home, Our Story,
  Sustainability, Help in brand voice. Create the **lead magnet**.
- **Week 3:** Build the **welcome + cart-abandon flows**. Publish 2 Journal
  articles. Set up Instagram + Pinterest, seed first 9 posts + 30 pins.
- **Week 4:** **Soft launch** to a warm circle (friends, family, existing
  contacts). Gather first orders + **reviews + photos**. Fix friction found.

### Month 2 — Grow the audience
**Goal: list growth + organic traffic.**

- Public launch announcement (email + social + the founder's story).
- Turn on the **content engine**: 1 pillar idea / 2 weeks → article + social +
  newsletter (every 2 weeks).
- Pinterest cadence steady (5–10/week). Instagram 3×/week + 1–2 reels/week.
- Reach out to **5–10 taste-aligned micro-influencers** with gifted pieces.
- Start **retargeting ads** (small budget) to site visitors.
- Collect + display reviews; start a UGC habit (re-share customer photos).

### Month 3 — Optimise & scale what works
**Goal: profitable, repeatable acquisition.**

- Review the **5 numbers** weekly; double down on the best channel.
- A/B test homepage hero, product page, and the welcome offer.
- Expand Journal (target 6–8 live articles for SEO momentum).
- Scale only the **profitable** paid campaigns; add Google Shopping if intent is
  there.
- Launch the first **seasonal/gifting bundle** (raises AOV).
- First **PR push** with the press kit.
- Plan Phase 2: the Interior Service (e-design) lead-capture page.

## 5. Metrics — the weekly dashboard

Track **five numbers** weekly; ignore vanity metrics:

| Metric | Why it matters | Early target (set with founder) |
|---|---|---|
| **Email sign-ups** | The compounding owned asset | Steady weekly growth |
| **Sessions** | Top-of-funnel demand | Growing, mostly organic |
| **Conversion rate** | Is the funnel working? | Benchmark ~1.5–2.5%+, then improve |
| **Revenue** | The result | Bottom-up target (see 05) |
| **Repeat-purchase rate** | Loyalty / LTV health | Rising over time |

Monthly, also review: **CAC, LTV:CAC, AOV, channel mix.**

> ⚠️ Targets must be set **with the founder** against real capacity, budget, and
> any current baseline. Benchmarks above are industry-typical starting points.

## 6. Budget shape (illustrative — confirm real numbers)

Early-stage, the spend mix should favour **owned/earned over paid**:

| Area | Share of early budget | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Photography/video** | Largest one-off | The asset everything draws from |
| **Email/tools** (Klaviyo, reviews) | Small recurring | Highest ROI; non-negotiable |
| **Content creation** | Ongoing | The engine; mostly time, some production |
| **Paid ads** | Small, growing only when profitable | Retargeting first |
| **Influencer/PR** | Mostly product cost (gifting) | Borrowed trust, cheap |

> Principle: **earn attention before you buy it.** A strong organic + email base
> makes every future ad euro work harder (warm audiences convert cheaper).

## 7. The 10 rules (pin these above the desk)

1. Grow the **email list** every single week.
2. **Owned and earned** before paid.
3. One **pillar idea per fortnight**, atomised into everything.
4. **Photography first** — it's the product online.
5. **Calm and consistent** beats loud and sporadic.
6. **Never discount** the brand into the ground — bundle and add value instead.
7. Reply to **every** comment, DM, and email — warmly, like a human.
8. **Pinterest = long-life traffic.** Keep pinning.
9. Turn buyers into **storytellers** (reviews, UGC, referrals).
10. Watch **five numbers**, ignore the vanity ones.
