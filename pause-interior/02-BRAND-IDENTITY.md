## 1. Why a brand system exists

A brand is not a logo. It is the **consistent feeling** a customer gets across
every touchpoint — the site, an email, a package on the doorstep, a reel at
11pm. For a small brand, consistency is the thing that makes it _feel_ big,
trustworthy, and premium. This document is the rulebook that lets five different
people (or one founder on five different days) produce work that all feels like
**one calm voice**.

> **The brand in one line:** _Pause Interior is the feeling of a calmer home._

## 3. Verbal identity (voice & tone)

Voice is constant; tone flexes with context. Pause's voice is **calm, warm,
confident, and unhurried.**

### 3.1 The five voice principles

1. **Speak calmly.** Short sentences. Room to breathe. Never a wall of text,
   never an exclamation storm. The way we write should feel the way the brand
   feels.
2. **Lead with the feeling, support with the craft.** Open with what it does for
   the customer's home and life; then earn it with the material, the maker, the
   detail. _(Feeling first, proof second.)_
3. **Be warm, not salesy.** We invite; we never pressure. No fake urgency, no
   "BUY NOW", no manufactured scarcity. Trust is the asset; pressure spends it.
4. **Be specific and honest.** "Solid European oak, oiled by hand" beats
   "premium quality materials". Specifics build trust; vague superlatives erode
   it. This is also how we avoid greenwashing.
5. **Less, but better — in words too.** Cut every word that isn't earning its
   place. White space is a feature of our writing, not just our design.

### 3.2 Tone by context

| Context | Tone dial |
|---|---|
| Homepage / hero | Evocative, emotional, spare |
| Product description | Sensory + specific; feeling then facts |
| Email newsletter | Personal, story-led, like a note from a friend |
| Transactional email | Warm, clear, reassuring |
| Customer service | Calm, generous, human — solve, don't defend |
| Social caption | Quiet, observational, a small thought |
| Error / out-of-stock | Honest, gentle, never apologetic-to-excess |

### 3.3 Words we use / words we avoid

**Use:** pause · calm · breathe · home · timeless · natural · honest · warm ·
crafted · lasting · considered · gather · slow · light · linen · oak · wool ·
stone · made to last · designed in Sweden.

**Avoid:** cheap · deal · hurry · limited time · must-have · trendy · luxury
(show it, don't claim it) · disrupt · revolutionary · exclusive (as hype) · BUY
NOW · sale!!! · game-changer.

### 3.4 Taglines & signature lines

- **Primary:** _Pause. You're home._
- **Promise line:** _Fewer, better things._
- **Descriptor (for bios/SEO):** _Timeless Scandinavian interior, made to last._
- **Sign-off (emails):** _Take a pause,_ / _Warmly, [Name] · Pause Interior_

> ⚠️ **CONFIRM** which lines already exist on the live site so we keep what
> customers already recognise.

### 3.5 Micro-copy examples (the brand, applied)

| Moment | ❌ Off-brand | ✅ On-brand |
|---|---|---|
| Add to cart | "ADD TO CART — HURRY!" | "Add to your home" |

## 4. Visual identity

> ⚠️ This section is a **recommended system**. Confirm the founder's existing
> logo, colours, and fonts first and adapt — consistency with what customers
> already see beats a clean-slate rebuild.

### 4.1 Design principles

- **White space is the hero.** Generosity, not density. Let everything breathe.
- **Materials, not graphics.** The texture of oak, linen, and stone is the
  decoration. Avoid heavy graphic devices, gradients, hard shadows.
- **Tonal, natural palette.** Warm neutrals, soft contrast. Nothing shouts.
- **Photography leads.** The visual brand is carried mostly by imagery (§5);
  graphics stay quiet and supportive.
- **Quiet typography.** Elegant, legible, restrained. Type sets a mood, it
  doesn't perform.

### 4.2 Colour system (recommended — confirm against current)

A warm, calm, natural palette. Confirm exact hexes against the live brand.

| Role | Name | Hex (suggested) | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base / background | **Linen White** | `#F6F2EC` | Primary background, breathing space |
| Surface | **Warm Sand** | `#EDE6DA` | Cards, secondary surfaces |
| Ink / text | **Soft Charcoal** | `#2B2A28` | Body text, headings (never pure black) |
| Accent / earth | **Clay** | `#B8A68F` | Subtle accents, dividers |
| Deep accent | **Forest Stone** | `#52584E` | Buttons, emphasis, sparingly |
| Pure | **Off-White** | `#FBF9F5` | Cards over base, highlights |

> **Rule:** never pure black (`#000`) or pure white (`#FFF`). Everything is
> slightly warm and soft. That single rule already makes a site feel calmer.

### 4.3 Typography (recommended pairing)

- **Headings:** an elegant **serif** with character but restraint — conveys
  timelessness and craft. _(e.g. a refined transitional/old-style serif.)_
- **Body & UI:** a clean, humanist **sans-serif** — calm, highly legible, modern
  Scandinavian. _(e.g. a neutral grotesque/humanist sans.)_
- **Hierarchy:** generous line-height (1.5–1.7 body), wide margins, large type
  sizes. Let text sit in space.

⚠️ **CONFIRM** the founder's current fonts; if they're already chosen, we keep
them and define the scale around them.

### 4.4 Logo usage (principles)

- Give the logo **clear space** — at least the height of the logo on all sides.
- Use the **monochrome** (Soft Charcoal or Off-White) versions; avoid colour
  logos on busy photos.
- Never stretch, recolour outside the palette, add effects, or crowd it.
- Provide a **favicon / app-icon** and a small "mark" version for social avatars.

⚠️ **CONFIRM** logo files exist in: full lockup, mark-only, light, and dark.

## 5. Photography & art direction — _the single most important asset_

For an interior brand sold online, **photography is the product experience.** It
is where most of the budget and care should go. A modest collection shot
beautifully outperforms a large collection shot poorly.

### 5.1 The look

- **Natural light only**, soft and directional. Morning/late-afternoon warmth.
  No hard flash, no studio glare.
- **Real, lived-in rooms** — not sterile showrooms. A throw slightly creased, a
  cup on the table, light across a wall. _Calm, not empty._
- **Tonal & quiet** — backgrounds in the brand palette; nothing competes with
  the piece.
- **Texture in close-up** — the grain of oak, the weave of linen, the glaze of
  ceramic. Tactility sells natural materials.
- **Negative space** — room around every object. Space _is_ the brand.
- **Human, but barely** — a hand, a silhouette, a moment of stillness. People as
  warmth, not as fashion models.

### 5.2 The three shot types (every product needs all three)

1. **Hero / lifestyle** — the piece in a calm, real room (emotional; sells the
   feeling).
2. **Studio / clean** — the piece on a tonal background, edges clear (functional;
   for the grid and detail).
3. **Detail / macro** — material, joint, texture, finish (trust; proves quality).

### 5.3 Video / motion

Short, slow, ambient: light moving across a room, fabric settling, a candle lit,
hands placing a vase. **No fast cuts, no trending audio at full volume.** Motion
should lower the viewer's heart rate. These clips fuel Instagram/Pinterest reels
and the homepage.

> ⚠️ **Recommendation:** the **first investment** after this strategy is a proper
> photo + video shoot of the core collection in the right light. It is the asset
> the website, ads, email, and social all draw from for years.

## 6. Brand applications (consistency in the wild)

| Touchpoint | The brand shows up as… |
|---|---|
| **Website** | Generous white space, big calm imagery, serif headlines, soft palette, unhurried copy |
| **Packaging** | Understated, natural/recycled materials, a handwritten-feel note: _"Take a pause."_ |
| **Unboxing** | Quiet ritual — tissue, twine, a card with the story of the piece |
| **Email** | Lots of white space, one beautiful image, a short story, one gentle CTA |
| **Instagram grid** | A calm, tonal mosaic — feels like one continuous mood |
| **Customer service** | Warm, generous, human; treats problems as a chance to deepen trust |
| **Invoices/transactional** | Even the receipt feels considered and warm |

> The unboxing and after-care are not "operations" — they are **marketing**.
> They create the photos customers post and the word of mouth the whole funnel
> depends on (see 04-MARKETING-PLAYBOOK §referral).

## 7. The brand consistency checklist

Before anything ships (a page, a post, an email, a product), check:

- [ ] Does it make someone feel **calmer**, not busier?
- [ ] Is the **feeling** leading, with craft as proof?
- [ ] Is the copy **short, warm, specific, honest** — no hype, no false urgency?
- [ ] Is there enough **white space** (visually and verbally)?
- [ ] Is the **photography** natural-light, tonal, real, with room to breathe?
- [ ] Are colours within the **warm, soft palette** (no pure black/white)?
- [ ] Would **"she"** (§2) say it this way?
- [ ] Does it deepen the promise — _fewer, better things_ — or dilute it?

> If any box is unchecked, it isn't ready. This one checklist is how a small team
> protects a premium brand at scale.
